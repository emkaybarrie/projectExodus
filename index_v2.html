Why MyFi exists

Money is emotional before it is numerical. Most tools assume people need more information; MyFi assumes they need a calmer rhythm, a simple signal and a space that doesn’t shame them for struggling.

The mission is to build a prevention-first “behavioural wellbeing layer” that can sit between advice services, councils, universities and banks – helping people feel protected long before crisis.

Prevention, not panic

Designed for early warning and small nudges, not red-alert moments.

Dignity by default

Neutral language, no shaming, and controls that protect essentials.

Playful, but serious

A fantasy aesthetic and game loops wrapped around real-world safety.

About MyFi

How the Behaviour Engine and Vitals System turn real-world money into something you can actually read at a glance.

Health

Emergency safety & resilience – your ability to survive shocks.

Mana

Intentional, “power” spending for planned moves and big decisions.

Stamina

Day-to-day spending rhythm; how quickly you burn through your month.

Essence

Long-term potential crystallised from consistent underspending.

Emberward

Shielded essentials – rent, bills, non-negotiables that must be protected.

Partnerships

MyFi is designed to sit between advice, public sector and banks – a shared prevention layer everyone can stand behind.

CAB & Advice

Gentle, non-shaming space where clients can stabilise their relationship with money.

Councils & Public Sector

Prevention-first tool for residents and young people; supports cost-of-living and resilience agendas.

Banks & FS

Behavioural wellbeing engine that supports Consumer Duty, engagement and ESG without selling credit.

One engine, many ways to plug in

Overlay

Partner funds access and signposts into the standalone MyFi app.

Embedded

Vitals view dropped into existing apps via webview or SDK.

Engine API

Partner sends data; MyFi returns vitals and states for in-house UI.

Why we’re building MyFi

MyFi grew out of years of working with complex financial systems and watching people feel crushed by them. The vision is to create a space where money feels understandable, where mistakes don’t define you, and where banks and public bodies can genuinely help people stay out of harm’s way.
